Be aware of Eldritch Creatures

The word "eldritch" means something like "coming from a foreign realm". These beings are the strangest the universe has to offer; even for alien standards they're otherworldly. They are usually extremely powerful and typically display unexplainable and supernatural abilities.

They may manifest themselves either corporeally or noncorporeally. In some cases, their physical manifestations constitute a mere shadow of what they truly are, and they may have several avatars.

Often, these beings are neutral towards lesser life forms, but they might be extremely malevolent, or in rare cases they might be friendly.

Be aware of The Multi-Bear

A nameless abomination, a testament to my failures - it must be destroyed!

The Multi-Bear is a large black bear with eight heads and twelve limbs. He has one main head, capable of human speech and possessing the prime brain of the creature, which is surrounded by four other heads on his neck region. He has three other heads, one on his back attached to a partially formed separate body, and one in each of his two crotches.

He has two sets of arms, and two sets of legs, as well as an additional, vestigial set of limbs on the partially formed body on his back. He is capable of standing upright on his multiple legs, as well as crouching down on all eight of his main limbs (excluding the four on his back) in the same fashion as a normal bear would.

On a closer look the Multi-Bear seems to have three separate bodies: two at the front and one at the back. Each of the two front bodies has a head on either shoulder and one in the crotch, while the backmost body has only a single head, and all three are attached to the main head on top. As each body has four limbs it all totals up to eight heads and twelve limbs.

Be aware of "The Collector"

"Be aware of "Ancient Creatures"

The twisted faces of the damned, piled high and cloaked in malice!

Get used to fighting the Collector, as it is bound to show up numerous times during the campaign. The problem is that since you can't predict when you'll run into it, you're probably not prepared for it when you do. This means using whatever party build you have on hand at the time, to try to fight the thing. The good news is that even if you are forced to abandon the quest afterwards, the gems will make it worth your trouble.

When the fight starts, try to stun the Collector. It does not have high stun resist and this can give you 2 turns to attack before any minions are summoned. The Collector, unlike most bosses, has all around weak stats. The only thing it has going for it is dodge, and even that isn't very high.

After it summons minions, it'll be in the back slot and harder to reach. You can either pull it forward, snipe it, or try to clear to it. Clearing through the minions however won't grant you much time before they are replaced.

Since it will keep replacing the minions, it's ideal to ignore them as much as possible. This is not too hard to do with the Man At Arms and Vestal, which mostly just drag out the fight with supportive skills. The Highwayman however has too much damage to ignore. This one is best to be killed whenever it's active.

The Collector doesn't have much offense on its own, but its main attack will heal itself while marking the target, also attracting Highwayman. Both can apply bleeds as well, so bandages are recommended to remove these. Since the Collector and all minions are only 1 slot, multiple target skills can be effective in the fight.

Whenever the Collector is slain, the minions will disappear.
